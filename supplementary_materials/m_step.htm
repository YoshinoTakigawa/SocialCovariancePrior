M-step in Variational Expectation Maximization

Description

M-step in VEM is to evaluate the values of model parameters in \( \theta \).

Since we do not understand the true distribution of likelihood \( p(R | \theta) \), we are disabled to learn true \( \theta \). However, by Jensen's Inequality, we have a lower bound \( L \) of the log likelihood. The lower bound provides an indirect way to evaluate the values in \( \theta \).

We can rewrite \( L \) to eliminate unimportant terms to speed up analyses. \[ \begin{align} \log p(R | \theta) & \geq \E_{q(Z | \theta')} \left [ \log \frac{p(R, Z | \theta)}{q(Z | \theta')} \right ] & \because \text{ Jensen's Inequality} \\ & = \E_{q(Z | \theta')} \left [ \log p(R, Z | \theta) \right ] - \E_{q(Z | \theta')} \left [ \log q(Z | \theta') \right ] & \because \text{ properties of } \log, \E \\ \end{align} \] Clearly, the second term (entropy of \( q(Z | \theta') \)) is not relevant to \( \theta \). Hence, we just extend the first term to find the close-form solutions of variables in \( \theta \).
